What we are building, and how it runs

Two parts. First the product topics across MVP and the phases after. Then the actual way the work goes, lane by lane, as live Claude sessions.

Part 1 · Product roadmap

North star: Behavioral OS. We show how your behaviors connect, sleep against mood against screen time and more. It starts in the MVP (we capture multiple behaviors) and deepens every phase.

MVP

The 50 founding users
Voice-first onboarding that works The AI fills the app (tool-calling) Solid, shippable mobile app Founding sign-up on the website Notifications + email sequence Thierry MVP additions (weekdays, completion types, notes, free trial, drop-off analytics) Some insights (basic progress + history) Behavioral OS foundation Safety + privacy baseline (988 boundary) QA pass before launch

Phase 1

Right after launch · depth
Insights improve (trends, deeper history) Coaching personalization (style selector, morning context) Behavioral OS grows (first connections surfaced)

Phase 2

Differentiation
Sleep + Apple Health / Google Health Wearables Screen time category Voice-controlled settings Gym / power-user logging Accountability (groups, challenges, seeing others) UI depth (animations, real chat screens, visual cards) Competitor study Behavioral OS deepens (cross-behavior correlations)

Phase 3

Expansion + bets
Category packs (nutrition, fitness, womens health) Marketplace (human-coach handoff, supplements) Voice infra evolution (self-hosted Pipecat)

Part 2 · How it runs, lane by lane

The model: one lane per person. Each lane holds that person's projects as Claude session buckets. Each bucket is an ordered task queue. You keep 2 to 3 running as live sessions, jump between them, the session does the next task and moves on. Product is the planning. Engineering is all the execution (Yonas). QA is checking (Timothy).
